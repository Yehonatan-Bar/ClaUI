The First Law — Before They Are Hanged

The desert had stripped her down to truths
sharp as broken stone: thirst, pain, and the
names of men worth killing. Everything else
was comfort, and comfort was a lie.

"Mercy is for people who still have
something left to spare."

She trusted hard ground, quick hands, and
the hatred that had kept her breathing when
kinder feelings would have left her in chains
or buried under the sand.

"I did not survive
to forgive."

Let them call her devil.
They forged her from cruelty
and taught her its language.

She walked beside magi and killers alike because power
might yet purchase vengeance, and vengeance was the only
future she had ever trusted.

Ferro Maljinn
Devil-Blood
