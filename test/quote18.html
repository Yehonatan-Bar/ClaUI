The Burning of the Ships

Then Fëanor, mightiest of the Noldor,
whose works were held in wonder,
crafted three jewels: the Silmarils.

The inner fire of the Silmarils
Fëanor made of the blended light
of the Trees of Valinor,
which lives in them yet, though the Trees
have long withered and shine no more.

Then Fëanor swore a terrible oath.
His seven sons leapt to his side
and took the selfsame vow together,
and red as blood shone their drawn swords.

They swore an oath which none shall break,
and none should take, by the name of
Ilúvatar, calling the Everlasting Dark
upon them if they kept it not.

And Fëanor set fire to the white ships
of the Teleri. So in that place which was called
Losgar at the mouth of the Firth of Drengist
ended the fairest vessels that ever sailed the sea,
in a great burning, bright and terrible.

And Fingolfin and his people saw the light afar off,
red beneath the clouds; and they knew
that they were betrayed.
This was the first of the fruits
of the Kinslaying and the Doom of the Noldor.
