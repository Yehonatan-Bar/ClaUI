The First Law — Before They Are Hanged

The mirrors of Adua had always been kind.
A straight back, a polished coat, a smile
rehearsed in candlelight; they could make
vanity look perilously close to virtue.

"A man can live a long time on applause,
if no one has yet asked him for courage."

But campaign roads, cold camps, and ugly
choices had a way of scraping gilt from the soul.
Underneath the shine there had to be something
sturdier than pride, or nothing at all.

"Glory is only noise.
The hard part is becoming worth the noise."

Rank lends shine.
Suffering lends shape.

The Union taught him how to bow in the right rooms.
Defeat taught him how to stand when no one was looking.

Jezal dan Luthar
Officer of the King's Own
