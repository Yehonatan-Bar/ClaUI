The First Law — The Blade Itself

Why do I do this?

The stairs were the worst part. Every step
sent a lance of fire through his ruined leg,
from hip to toe. He clutched the banister
with white-knuckled fingers and dragged
himself upward, one agonising step at a time.

"Body found floating by the docks.
Water in the lungs. Water in the stomach.
But that proves nothing, of course."

We all have our pain. I have mine and
you will have yours, before this is done.

"We should forgive our enemies,
but not before they are hanged."

He paused on the landing, his leg
screaming, his gums throbbing where
his teeth had been, and smiled his
toothless, ruined, merciless smile.

That's what Superior Glokta did.
He made people afraid.
He asked the questions no one else
was willing to ask.

And why? Because I have nothing left
to lose. A cripple has few fears.

Sand dan Glokta
Superior of the Inquisition
