The First Law — The Blade Itself

Logen plunged through the trees, bare feet
slipping and sliding on the wet earth —
the North roaring behind him with a voice
of blood and broken shields.

"Once you've got a task to do,
it's better to do it than live
with the fear of it."

He had fought in three campaigns. He had
fought single combats and he had survived.
Not because he was the strongest or the fastest.

Say one thing for Logen Ninefingers,
say he's a survivor.

And when the blade grew heavy in his hand,
when hope was thin as winter ice —
he would whisper the only truth
the North had ever taught him:

"You have to be realistic
about these things."

Logen Ninefingers
The Bloody-Nine
