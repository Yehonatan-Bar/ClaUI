The Silmarillion — Of the Beginning of Days

In seven hours the glory of each tree
waxed to full and waned again to naught;
and each awoke once more to life
an hour before the other ceased to shine.
Thus in Valinor twice every day
there came a gentle hour of softer light
when both trees were faint and their
gold and silver beams were mingled.
